Speaker setup
Here is the condensed, step-by-step summary for manually setting up your Onkyo TX-NR595 for your 7.1 ceiling-speaker layout, including the decibel calibration.
1. Initial Configuration & Impedance
Before dialing in the sound, tell the receiver what it is powering.
* Impedance: Go to 2.1 Configuration > Speaker Impedance and ensure it is set to 6 ohms (the safe, default setting for your 8-ohm Polk speakers).
* Layout: In the same menu, set Speaker Channels to 7.1 ch.
* Subwoofer: Set to Yes.
2. Crossovers & Distances
This routes the bass correctly and ensures the audio reaches your ears at the exact same time.
* Crossovers (2.2 Crossover): Set Front, Center, Surround, and Surround Back all to 80Hz. Set the LPF of LFE (subwoofer channel) to 120Hz.
* Distance (2.3 Distance): Sit in your exact primary viewing seat. Use a tape measure to measure the distance from your head to the face of every single speaker (and the subwoofer). Enter these exact numbers into the menu.
3. The Decibel Check (Level Calibration)
This is the most important step for a balanced, immersive bubble.
* Download a free Decibel (dB) meter app on your phone.
* Navigate to 2.4 Level Calibration on the Onkyo.
* Sit in your primary seat and hold the phone upright right in front of your face (ear level).
* The receiver will output a static test tone for each speaker. Adjust the volume in the Onkyo menu until the app hits your targets.
Decibel Calibration Targets:
Speaker Channel	Target Level	Adjustment Reason
Front L/R & Surrounds	75 dB	This is your baseline. All standard speakers should be equal.
Center Channel	76 dB to 78 dB	Bumping this up +1 to +3 dB ensures movie dialogue is always clear over the action.
Subwoofer	78 dB to 81 dB	Running the sub "hot" by +3 to +6 dB gives you that cinematic punch, and compensates for phone mics being bad at reading bass.
Ceiling Speakers	73 dB to 74 dB	(Optional) If the ceiling speakers are directly over your head and feel distracting, drop them -1 to -2 dB to blend them back in.
