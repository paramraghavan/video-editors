# Insta360 X3 Best Video Settings

## My Simple Recommendation

### For Most Family Events

Use this when you do **not want to miss the action** and want to reframe the video later.

**Use:**

`360 Video → 5.7K 30fps → Standard color → Reframe later`

### For Quick Normal Camcorder Use

Use this when you want a **normal flat video**, similar to a regular camcorder or phone video.

**Use:**

`Single Lens → 4K 30fps → Horizontal → Wide or Linear`

---

# Option 1: Family Events — 360 Video, 5.7K 30fps

Use this for:

- School programs
- Graduation
- Temple events
- Dance performances
- Stage programs
- Family parties
- Sports events

This is the safest setting because the camera records everything around you. Later, you can choose the best angle in the
Insta360 app or Insta360 Studio.

## Steps on the Insta360 X3 Camera

1. **Turn on the X3.**

2. On the main screen, tap the **Switch Lens icon** at the **lower-right corner**.

3. Select **360 Mode**.

4. Make sure the shooting mode is **Video**.

5. Tap the **resolution / frame-rate setting** at the bottom of the screen.

6. Select:

   `5.7K → 30fps`

7. Swipe **left from the right edge** of the screen to open video settings.

8. Set:

    - **Color:** Standard
    - **White Balance:** Auto
    - **Exposure:** Auto

9. Press the **red record button**.

## How to Hold It for Events

Hold the camera at **chest or face height**.

You do not need to aim perfectly because 360 mode records all directions. Later, you can reframe and choose the best
angle.

## Best Event Preset

`360 Mode → Video → 5.7K 30fps → Standard color → Auto exposure → Auto white balance`

# Option 2: Normal Camcorder Style — Single Lens, 4K 30fps

Use this when you want a **normal video file** that looks like it was recorded using a phone or camcorder.

## Steps on the Insta360 X3 Camera

1. **Turn on the X3.**

2. Tap the **Switch Lens icon** at the **lower-right corner**.

3. Select **Single Lens**.

4. Make sure the shooting mode is **Video**.

5. Tap the **resolution / frame-rate setting** at the bottom of the screen.

6. Select:

   `4K → 30fps`

7. Choose the **aspect ratio**:

    - **16:9** for normal horizontal camcorder video
    - **9:16** for vertical phone / Instagram video

8. Choose the **Field of View**:

    - **Linear** = most natural, less fisheye look
    - **Wide** = captures more of the room or stage
    - Avoid **UltraWide** if you want a normal camcorder-style video

9. Swipe **left from the right edge** of the screen.

10. Set:

- **Color:** Standard
- **White Balance:** Auto
- **Exposure:** 0

11. Press the **red record button**.

## Best Normal Camcorder Preset

`Single Lens → Video → 4K 30fps → 16:9 → Linear or Wide → Standard color`

## Use 360 Mode, 5.7K 30fps for Events

Best for:

- School programs
- Graduation
- Temple events
- Dance performances
- Stage performances
- Family parties

Recommended setting:

`360 Mode → 5.7K 30fps → Standard color → Reframe later`

## Use Single Lens, 4K 30fps for Normal Camcorder Video

Best for:

- Simple walking videos
- Talking videos
- Quick family clips
- Normal handheld recording

Recommended setting:

`Single Lens → 4K 30fps → 16:9 → Linear`

# Final Recommendation

For most events, use:

`360 Mode → 5.7K 30fps → Standard color`

This gives you the most flexibility because you can adjust the camera angle later.

For simple camcorder-style video, use:

`Single Lens → 4K 30fps → 16:9 → Linear`
