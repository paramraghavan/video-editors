**Subwoofer**

The Klipsch R-12SWi packs a serious punch, and since it is wireless, you have great flexibility on where to place it in the room.
Because your Onkyo receiver is doing all the heavy lifting for the bass management (handling the crossovers digitally in the receiver's menu), you need to set the physical dials on the back of the subwoofer to get out of the receiver's way.
Here is exactly how to set the physical controls on the back of your Klipsch sub:
1. The Dials
* GAIN (Volume): Turn this dial to 12 o'clock (pointing straight up, right in the middle). Right now, yours is a little low (around 9 o'clock). Setting it to 50% gives your Onkyo receiver a perfect baseline to work with when you do your decibel level calibration. You can always turn it up a notch later if you want to rattle the walls more!
* LOW PASS (Hz): Turn this dial all the way to the right (past 120Hz to the "LFE" setting).
    * Why: In your Onkyo menu, you already set the crossovers to 80Hz and the LPF of LFE to 120Hz. If you set the dial on the back of the sub too low, the sub will physically block the bass that the receiver is trying to send it. Turning it all the way up opens the gate completely, letting the Onkyo dictate exactly what frequencies the sub plays.
2. The Switches
* Power (ON / AUTO): Leave this set to AUTO (the middle position). This is perfect. The subwoofer will stay in a low-power standby mode and automatically wake up the second it senses bass coming from your Onkyo receiver.
* PHASE: Leave this set to 0 (pointing down).
    * Why: Phase aligns the in-and-out movement of the subwoofer cone with your front speakers. For 90% of setups where the sub is near the front of the room, 0 is the correct setting. You would only flip this to 180 if the subwoofer was placed entirely behind your seating area and sounded "hollow" or out of sync.
Set those dials, and your Klipsch is perfectly primed to take orders from your Onkyo!
