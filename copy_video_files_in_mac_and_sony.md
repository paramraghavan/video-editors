# How to Read and Play Sony AVCHD Files on macOS

Sony AVCHD videos are stored as **hidden individual clips (`.mts` files)** wrapped inside a camera file bundle. You can
either extract the clips using Finder or import them directly into video editing applications such as **iMovie**.

## Method 1: Extract Video Files via Finder

1. Insert your Sony SD card or connect your camera to your Mac using a USB cable.
2. Open **Finder**.
3. Under **Locations**, select your camera or SD card.
4. Open the `PRIVATE` folder.
5. Open the `AVCHD` folder.
6. Right-click (or **Control-click**) the `AVCHD` file and choose **Show Package Contents**.
7. Inside, locate the `BDMV` folder.
8. Right-click (or **Control-click**) `BDMV` and choose **Show Package Contents**.
9. Open the `STREAM` folder.
10. You will see the individual video files with the `.mts` extension.
11. Drag the `.mts` files to your Mac to copy them.

## Alternative: Import Directly into iMovie

1. Connect your camera or insert the SD card.
2. Open **iMovie**.
3. Choose **Import Media**.
4. Select your camera or SD card.
5. Import the desired AVCHD clips into your project.

This method preserves the original video quality and avoids manually extracting the `.mts` files.
