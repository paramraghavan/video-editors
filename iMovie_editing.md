# 🎬 iMovie Editing Cheat Sheet

## 1. Start a Project

* Open **iMovie** → click **Create New** → **Movie**.
* Import media: drag & drop video, photos, or audio into the **Media Library**.

---

## 2. Build Your Timeline

* Drag clips from Media Library → **Timeline (bottom bar)**.
* Arrange clips left to right (order of play).
* Shorten/trim: drag edges of a clip in/out.
* Split clip: select → **Cmd + B** (or right-click → Split).

---

## 3. Add Transitions

* Click **Transitions (top bar)**.
* Drag transition (e.g., Cross Dissolve, Fade) between clips.

---

## 4. Add Titles & Text

* Click **Titles (top bar)**.
* Drag a title style into timeline above video.
* Edit text in the **Preview Window**.

---

## 5. Add Music or Sound

* Click **Audio** (or import your own MP3).
* Drag into **Timeline (below video track)**.
* Adjust volume: click audio → drag volume line up/down.
* Fade in/out: drag little circle at start/end of audio.

---

## 6. Add Effects

* Select a clip → use **Adjust toolbar (above preview)**:

  * 🎨 **Color correction** → brighten, contrast, etc.
  * 🔊 **Audio** → noise reduction, EQ.
  * 🔍 **Crop/Zoom** → Fit, Crop to Fill, Ken Burns effect.

---

## 7. Export (Share Your Movie)

* Click **Share (top right)** → **File**.
* Choose resolution (1080p is good).
* Save → ready to upload or share.

---

# ⏱️ Keyboard Shortcuts (Time Savers)

* **Cmd + B** → Split clip
* **Cmd + Z** → Undo
* **Spacebar** → Play/Pause
* **Cmd + +/-** → Zoom timeline in/out
* **Delete** → Remove selected clip

---

✨ With just **Import → Arrange → Trim → Titles → Music → Export**, you can make a clean video in minutes.


# Quick iMovie Edit

Making **iMovie’s “Magic Movie” (sometimes just called “Magic Movie” or “Magic Movie 3”) feature
** on MacBook. Apple first rolled this out on **iOS/iPadOS (iMovie for iPhone/iPad)**, but on **Mac**, the closest
equivalent is **“Trailer”** or **“Themes”** in iMovie.

## 🖥️ Using iMovie on Mac for Quick Auto-Edits

Since Magic Movie itself is iOS-only:

1. **Open iMovie** on your Mac.
2. Click **Create New** → choose **Movie**.
3. **Import your engagement MP4**:

   * Drag the file in, or click **Import Media**.
4. **Apply a Theme (Auto-Edit Equivalent):**

   * Go to the top menu → **File → Project Properties**.
   * Pick a **Theme** (e.g., Celebration, Travel, News, etc.).
   * iMovie will **auto add titles, transitions, and music** in that style.
5. **Enable Auto-Content**:

   * When asked, check **Automatically add transitions & titles**.
6. **Fine Tune (Optional):**
   * Reorder clips, adjust music length, or swap the theme soundtrack with your own.

---

## 📱 Workaround: Use iPhone/iPad for Magic Movie, Then Edit on Mac

If you want the *true* “Magic Movie” experience:

1. On your iPhone/iPad, open **iMovie** → **Magic Movie**.
2. Select your engagement video (or multiple clips).
3. iMovie auto-builds a polished video with **music, titles, transitions**.
4. Export the finished project (or even the project file).
5. Open it in iMovie on your **MacBook** for further editing.

---

## ⚡ Quick Alternative on Mac

If you want even more automation than iMovie’s Themes:

* **CapCut desktop app** → “Auto Cut.”
* **Adobe Premiere Rush** → Auto Reframe.
  These feel more like “Magic Movie” on Mac and can instantly give you a highlight reel.
